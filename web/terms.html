Amber is a supportive companion, not a doctor. These terms explain what Amber is for, what it is not, and the ground rules for using it.

What Amber is

Amber is a companion app for people on an eMed weight-loss programme. It helps you log meals, weigh-ins, and habits, remembers what matters to you, and offers encouragement and conversation along the way.

Not medical advice

Amber does not diagnose, treat, or prescribe, and nothing it says is medical advice. It will never set a target weight or a pace, or grade your progress — that is between you and your prescriber. For clinical questions, medication, or anything urgent, contact your care team. If you are in crisis or think you may be in danger, contact your local emergency services immediately.

Using Amber responsibly

  • Amber is for your personal use as an enrolled member.
  • The information you give Amber should be your own and accurate.
  • Do not rely on Amber for decisions that need a clinician.
  • AI can be wrong. Treat its replies as support, not instruction.

Your content

What you log and share stays yours. You can export or delete it at any time from Settings. See our Privacy Policy for how your data is handled.
